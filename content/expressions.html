menuExpressions
Expression parsing in the deployer is significantly different to in most programming languages, and very different to the traumatic experience of trying to describe calculated values in Terraform or CloudFormation. The intent has always been to try and make it possible to describe "values" in the most natural way possible.
It is up to individual verbs and contexts to decide where expressions start and finish, and whether only a "single" token is to be used for an expression, or the rest of the line. (A "single" token describes something like a number of a string, but also covers any whole expression beginning with a parenthesis, bracket or brace.)
The first thing that happens with expression parsing is that the expression is broken down into sub-expressions based on the presence of parentheses, brackets, braces, commas and colons: in short, explicit grouping, lists and maps are processed before other expression elements.
Within each sub-expression, parsing works from left to right. Each symbol is identified as either a function, an operator, an identifier, a constant or a sub-expression. Functions and operators are essentially the same. Each of them is characterized as prefix, postfix or infix. Each of them has a precedence level, and the parsing algorithm compares the precedence appropriately. This is complicated, so it is most easily described by example.
